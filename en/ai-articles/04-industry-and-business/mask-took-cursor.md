# xAI on the next game of what?

xAI is slowly becoming an AI infrastructure company from an AI model company.

Renting power to Cursor is just the beginning. Amazon, Microsoft and Google are all renting to other companies and making huge profits. XAI has a lot of GPU, and it doesn't have to be for its own use, it can be rented.

This explains why Musk is digging through Cursor's engineers: building the foundations for Grok's programming capacity. Cursor's team is the best IA programmers, dig 'em up, run 'em up with xAI -- it's a closed loop.

If this closed loop runs through, XAI is not just AI Model, it's an integrated platform for AI + AI programming capabilities.

And Cursor? Cursor has a $50 billion valuation, an annualized income of over $6 billion, and a large number of corporate clients. But it has a fatal question: it doesn't have its own calculus.

All its model training depends on someone else - OpenAI, Anthropic, Google, now XAI. That means it's in the hands of others.

The logic of SpaceX/XAI acquisition Cursor is quite straightforward.

**xAI is smart and has no programming data. Cursor has programming data, no stable algorithm.**

The two merged, xAI became the sub-infrastructure of Cursor, and Cursor became the export of programming capacity for xAI. Musk has X, Grok, SpaceX, XAI, Neuralink-Cursor to complement the AI programming, and the entire ecology is tied together.

And don't forget, SpaceX is making space data centres. If this works, Cursor's AI programming model can run directly into space algorithms. It sounds science fiction, but it's SpaceX moving in the direction.

Cursor is now in the position that it is the strongest company in the AI programming field, but it is subject to human control.

Because Cursor knew that he couldn't do it, and now Exit is fine.

Anthropic has Claude Code, OpenAI has Codex, Google has Gemini Code. Everybody's trying to figure it out. Cursor, to remain competitive, has to have a big back.

XAI now gives it the ability to calculate, which is the first step. What's the second step? It's to turn "competence" into "capitalism."

For Cursor investors, the acquisition by xAI means exit. For Cursor's team, being acquired by xAI means that their product can run directly across Musk's entire ecology.

It's more sexy than being listed independently.

The greatest risk to this transaction is time.

If Cursor ' s income continues to grow at a high rate, the path to independent listing becomes clearer and it does not have to be sold. If XAI ' s space data centre is built more slowly than expected, the synergy of the merger is less obvious.

The issue of SpaceX/XAI acquisition of Cursor is not a question of whether or not, but of what terms and when.
