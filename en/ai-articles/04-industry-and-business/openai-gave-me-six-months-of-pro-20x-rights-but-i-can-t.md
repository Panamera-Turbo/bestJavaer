> Date: 2026-05-02

March 18, I received an open source benefit from OpenAI.

It's probably because I am.`bestJavaer`The author is entitled to the benefit of Codex for Open Source, i. e. six months of ChatGPT Pro.

In other words, OpenAI gave me six months Pro 20x.

![image-20260502165020202](https://cdn.jsdelivr.net/gh/doggaifan/picbed/image-20260502165020202.png)

Honestly, the first reaction must be happy.

After all, ChatGPT Pro 20x is 200 U a month, six months is 1, 200 U, it's not cheap. More importantly, it's not an award, it's not a wool, it's an official OpenAI plan for Open Source Defenders. As a person who has written open-source projects for many years, it's nice to suddenly receive this endorsement.

And then the question came up.

I can't.

This is not a matter of process and not of warehouse eligibility.

There's only one problem: ** I'm from mainland China, which is not on the OpenAI list of currently supported areas.**

Thus, even if the warehouse itself meets the conditions, the interest may not be activated.

OpenAI Support's final manual response largely confirms this judgment:

![image-20260502165151998](https://cdn.jsdelivr.net/gh/doggaifan/picbed/image-20260502165151998.png)

Such services and benefits may not be convertible if account numbers are based on or primarily visit areas that are not supported. In the future, I may have access to the relevant interests if I am physically present in the country or area of support and the account number is satisfactory at that time. They do not, however, guarantee re-issuance, extension, retention or extension of such alternatives.

![image-20260502165439391](https://cdn.jsdelivr.net/gh/doggaifan/picbed/image-20260502165439391.png)

In other words, this benefit is real.

But it's true to me that I can't get it now.

At first I thought I was wrong

My first judgment was simple: if OpenAI sent me, it should be.

My GitHub warehouse is not just a little toy.`bestJavaer`It's been open for years. Star and fork are on a scale. This project can be identified, indicating that the selection logic of OpenAI has at least swept my open source identity.

So I didn't start by thinking about regional restrictions.

I tried a lot of ways.

Change browser, change login status, check OpenAI accounts, identify GitHub, confirm whether the mail was officially sent and confirm whether the receipt is expired.

None of the last discoveries.

The real stuck point is the OpenAI service area limit.

This place is delicate.

Because when you get your rights mail, it gives you the psychological expectation, "You're qualified."

But when it does, the system looks not only at whether you have an open source contribution, but also at your account number, area, compliance.

This track-off is realistic:** Open-source eligibility is one threshold and regional availability is another. The former passed, not the latter.**

---

I asked for a round of AI auxiliaries

I'm leaving now, OpenAI official Support.

I started by replying to AI-Asisted support, which is an AI auxiliary passenger service.

Its answer was straightforward:

ChatGPT does not currently support mainland China, so neither ChatGPT Pro nor Pro-related Codex interests can be activated or used in mainland China. The use of services in the areas was never supported, and there was a risk that accounts would be suspended.

It also gives an official list of support areas:

https://platform.openai.com/docs/supported-countries

Seeing here, it's pretty clear.

But I'm still not happy.

It's not because I want to bypass the restrictions, it's because the right itself belongs to Openai. I want to confirm one question:

If I had actually gone to the country or region of support in the future, could that right have been restored?

Or are there any alternatives to compliance, such as API credits, extensions, qualification.

This question is not answered by the AI client service.

So I keep asking for a transfer.

I didn't find the right button.

Not GitHub account untagged.

It's not mail.

It's the worst part

The hard part is not how much it costs.

Of course, six months Pro must be worth money. But the real pain is that you have been systematically identified as an open source defender, and that the rights have come out, and finally, because the regional restrictions are stuck at the end.

It's kind of like you got a ticket, and the door confirmed it was real, and the security check told you that you couldn't get in.

You can't even say it doesn't make sense.

Because from the perspective of OpenAI, it has to comply with regional compliance.

But from the point of view of the open source authors, the experience is quite fragmented.

In particular, open source projects themselves do not have national boundaries.

The code is on GitHub, issue from the world, Star from the world, and contributors from the world. However, once an interest has been placed on a specific account number and service, it returns to the real issues of area, payment, access to compliance.

That's an awkward reality now for AI to serve.

Technology is globalized, not services.

# To other open-source authors

If you receive an OpenAI-like open source benefit, I suggest you take it easy.

Three things first.

Your mail wasn't officially sent.

Your ChatGPT account box is not the same as the application mailbox.

Your country or region is not in the OpenAI support list.

If you're in a supported area, follow the official process. Use the real account number, the real information, not the substitute.

If you're in unsupported areas, especially mainland China, I suggest not to try to exchange money repeatedly, nor to communicate with customers with false regional information.

The most stable approach is to open official support lists, give a true picture and then ask whether it can be postponed, reissued or replaced.

Probably not too optimistic.

But at least you can get a formal conclusion that you don't have to waste your time on all kinds of "genetic methods."
