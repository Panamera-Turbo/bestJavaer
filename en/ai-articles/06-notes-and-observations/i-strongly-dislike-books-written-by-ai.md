# I Strongly Dislike Books Written by AI

[English](./i-strongly-dislike-books-written-by-ai.md) | [Chinese Original](../../../ai-articles/06-notes-and-observations/%E6%88%91%E4%B8%AA%E4%BA%BA%E9%9D%9E%E5%B8%B8%E4%B8%8D%E7%90%86%E8%A7%A3%E7%94%9A%E8%87%B3%E6%9C%89%E4%BA%9B%E5%8E%8C%E6%81%B6%E7%94%A8%20AI%20%E5%86%99%E4%B9%A6%E7%9A%84%E3%80%82.md)

> Date: 2026-07-22

Personally, I don’t understand or even hate using AI to write books.

I think there was a big V before. I wrote several books using AI on hot topics such as OpenClaw, Hermes, and Claude Code.

Then it was also put on WeChat for reading. And he used this as a gimmick to make people think he was awesome.

I don’t quite understand this process. It seems to me that the absurdity mentioned by Camus has an extremely strong allusion to reality.

I don't know what you think.

I have always regarded books as the sustenance and sublimation of the noble spirit of the human spirit.

From elementary school to university to work, I think books have accompanied me on many journeys and are the habitat of my soul at night.

As a student, I greatly respect the help and guidance that books have given me on my growth path, allowing me to become a person with my own judgment.

But with AI, things seem to have changed.

Everything is going faster and faster.

Publishing a good book is extremely labor-intensive. I tell you this because I usually write articles, and I have probably written hundreds of articles, so I have some understanding of it.

The book is the condensation and connection of each chapter, and the resonance of each writing.

I think writing books with AI is a waste.

You can betray your spiritual corner in an effortless way without getting reprimanded or tortured.

But in the process, you will definitely lose something. Something you can’t see but is important.

I admit that the articles written by AI are indeed logically rigorous and compact in structure, but after reading them, it seems that there will be a betrayal of the Creator's basic base.

I, as a human being, need to accept books that others use AI to read to me.

This process is full of inequality, because the person who uses AI to write a book may not be more capable than you. He may just rush to write a book using AI without doing any review, and just wrap it up and sell it as dog meat.

And I think technical books are a kind of preaching and guidance, so I don’t think the above-mentioned people can play a role in leading and guiding.

I don't think that a book written by someone who knows nothing about AI will be of any help to your level.

To put it bluntly, everyone is playing with poor information.

AI will amplify your personal abilities, but it will also amplify your ugliness.

When AI can mass-produce 60% qualified technical documents, the market will be flooded with a large number of suture monsters that have not been chewed by the author's brain.

What readers fear most is when the author pretends to do it personally, but in fact AI does it for him, thereby grabbing the reader's trust and money.

If all books will eventually be drowned by AI mass-produced text, then I would rather read those stumblingly written works, but the sincerity of the author is revealed in every page.

This is my last obsession with creation and my last dignity as a reader.
